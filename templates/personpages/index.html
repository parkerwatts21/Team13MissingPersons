About our Team

What is Human Trafficking?

Human trafficking is a crime that exploits and coerces individuals, often for forced labor or sexual exploitation. It's a modern form of slavery affecting millions globally, including women, men, and children. It's a $32 billion per year industry, ranking second to drug trafficking as the most profitable transnational crime.

How does Human Trafficking Happen?

Human trafficking is exploitation that happens within or across borders. Victims are deceived or coerced into vulnerable situations such as false job offers or marriage proposals. Traffickers use abuse, threats, and coercion to maintain control and prevent escape.

 

 

 

 

Human Trafficking in Utah

Utah, located at a major western crossroads, is a suspected transit and victimization point for thousands of human trafficking victims. It ranks in the top 10 states for reported cases. Girls aged 15-17 constitute over 50% of identified victims, while boys under 12 account for about 40%. This project will focus on using these statistics for JSON data on missing persons cases found, aged 0-27, in Utah.

What we can do to Help

If you suspect human trafficking or are a victim of human trafficking, it is important to seek help from appropriate authorities and service providers. You can contact the National Human Trafficking Hotline at 1-888-373-7888 or the Utah Hotline 801-200-3443 for help or report suspicious activity to local law enforcement. It is crucial to prioritize the safety and well-being of potential victims and take steps to combat human trafficking in Utah and beyond.
